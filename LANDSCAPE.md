# Landscape Scan: Shift Planner vs. Emma + gig-worker finance tools
Date: 2026-07-06
Trigger: human spotted the Emma app (UK budgeting/aggregator) and thought it looked "useful and similar."
Scope: standalone scan per discovery-protocol skill — not a kill/keep/reshape call. Shift Planner already exists and has shipped (v9 frozen 2026-07-06).

## What Shift Planner actually does (from SCOPE.md/README.md)
Converts monthly financial obligations (bills, savings, one-off goals) into the weekly work hours needed to cover them, blended across multiple jobs, tax-adjusted across 9 countries (UK default), with a shift log and week-over-week coverage tracking. It is an *hours-needed-forward* planner for people whose income is hourly/variable (gig, shift, multi-job) — not a transaction-tracking budgeting app for people with a fixed salary.

## Gap / where Shift Planner actually sits

Emma is not a competitor to Shift Planner's niche, it looks similar only at a glance. Emma starts from **transactions already in your bank account** (via Open Banking) and works backward to categorize and budget them; it assumes a income is already known and mostly fixed (salary). Shift Planner starts from the opposite end: **obligations you haven't yet earned the money for**, and forward-projects the tax-adjusted hours across possibly-multiple hourly jobs needed to cover them before payday. No search result surfaced an Emma feature that does this forward hours-needed calculation, blends multiple job rates, or applies a per-country progressive tax model to an hourly wage.

The gig-specific tools (Rodeo, GigTax, Hurdlr, ShiftTracker) are closer in audience (hourly/gig workers) but solve adjacent problems: Rodeo optimizes *which platform to work for*, GigTax and Hurdlr solve *tax filing/liability after the fact*, ShiftTracker solves *true per-hour profitability after costs*. None of the five tools found convert a monthly bill list into a required weekly-hours target, blend that across multiple named jobs, or track week-over-week which specific bills got covered in priority order, that combination (bills-to-hours, tax-adjusted, multi-job, priority-ordered coverage tracking) did not appear anywhere in this search session.

The nearest single-feature analogs are generic "price-to-hours" calculators (TimePrice, Time to Earn Calculator) that convert a single purchase into hours of work; none of them handle recurring monthly obligations, multiple jobs, tax models, or week-over-week tracking, they are one-shot converters, not planners.
